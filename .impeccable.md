## Design Context

### Users
Developers who use AI agents (Claude, Copilot, etc.) to write software. They're technical, familiar with compilers and type systems, and care about correctness. They encounter the site while evaluating tools for their AI-assisted workflow. Context: likely at their desk, dark IDE open in the next tab.

### Brand Personality
Forward-looking, precise, austere. Vow is a tool that proves things — it doesn't sell, it demonstrates. The tone is confident without being flashy. Think: a theorem, not an advertisement.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Tone**: Austere/mathematical. The site should feel like a beautifully typeset proof — structured, precise, with clear logical flow and generous whitespace.
- **Theme**: Dark mode (light mode planned for later).
- **Brand color**: Orange `#e64b25` is locked (the Vow claw). It is the singular accent.
- **Anti-references**: MoonBit (moonbitlang.com) — standard docs/marketing site with promotional banners and busy layouts. Vow should feel like the opposite: restrained, deliberate, fewer words with more impact.
- **Physical metaphor**: A proof inscribed on dark stone in a future mathematics institute. Geometric, spare, warm only where the brand mark appears.

### Design Principles
1. **Prove, don't promote.** Show the code. Let contracts speak for themselves. No marketing fluff.
2. **Precision is the aesthetic.** Every spacing value, color, and typographic choice should feel measured and intentional.
3. **Restraint over decoration.** White space is the primary design element. Fewer elements, each with more weight.
4. **The contracts are the hero.** `requires`, `ensures`, `invariant` — these keywords should be the most visually prominent elements after the logo.
5. **Forward-looking, not futuristic.** Modern and clean, but not sci-fi. Geometric, not glowing.
